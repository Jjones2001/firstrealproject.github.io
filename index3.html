Germany had just gone through an extreme depression of their own, and Adolf came with promises of redemption and hope.
People often are entirely focused on the murderous aspect of Hitler’s time as leader of Germany. But as horrible the things that he allowed to happen under his reign were, he did pull his country out of a financial depression.
The Holocaust itself was a huge tragedy, probably one of the hugest of all times, considering it was a mass genocide, but there was triumph in the restructuring of Germany after a depression. Not at all saying that Hitler had a good structure at all, but his position of authority certainly pulled the German people out of what seemed like hopelessness (even though it ultimately dragged them back down into catastrophe).
